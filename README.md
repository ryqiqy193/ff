## Two Rate Options: Pick Your Strategy

Here's where it gets interesting. FixedFloat gives you two ways to execute trades, and your choice depends on how much risk you're willing to take for a better deal.

**Fixed Rate (1% fee):** You see a price, you lock it in for 10 minutes, and that's what you get. Period. If Bitcoin suddenly tanks while your transaction confirms, you still get the amount shown when you created the order. The catch? You pay 1% for this certainty, and you need to send the exact amount within those 10 minutes. If the market moves more than 1.2% before your transaction hits the blockchain, they'll ask if you want to continue at the new rate or get a refund.

**Float Rate (0.5% fee):** This one's for the gamblers. You pay half the commission, but the final rate gets set when your transaction receives the necessary confirmations. Market goes up? You win. Market goes down? Well, that's on you. The platform processes whatever the going rate is when everything confirms, typically within 10 minutes.

Both options charge network fees on top - those go to miners and validators, not FixedFloat. The platform's transparent about this, showing you the full breakdown before you commit.
